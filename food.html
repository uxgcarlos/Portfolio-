Food Runners

Helping reduce hunger and waste in San Francisco

Concept Project Helping Eliminate Hunger in San Francisco
My Team Three person UX Design team

Duration Two week design sprint
Methods Survey, Interviews, Secondary Research, Affinity Mapping, Archetypes, Journey Mapping, Storyboarding, User Flows, System Maps, Competitive analysis, Comparative analysis, Design studio, Prototyping, Wireframing

Overview

Food Runners is a leader in the in the effort to keep the edible food from being discarded. It’s mission is to help alleviate hunger is San Francisco, prevent waste, and help create community. Food Runners picks up excess perishable and prepared food from business such as restaurants, caterers, bakeries, hospitals, event planners, corporate cafeterias, hotels, and delivers it directly to neighborhood food programs.
While the core service is about ‘food rescue’, FoodRunners wants to expand its mission to include educating people from all walks of life about nutrition, food waste, food security, and sustainability.

The Problem

Users need an easier way to learn more about the food waste movement while also being able to find an easy ways to how to get involved.

The Solution

By giving the user easy, accessible, and trustworthy information this will give them an easier flow which will educate, spread awareness and enable them to proactively get involved in the food waste movement.

Key Takeaways

  • Collaboration and communication is crucial in creating the best product.
  • Affinity mapping is a great way to visualize trends and patterns.
  • Conducting interviews is a great way to get primary research on the users motivations which was very beneficial for our project.

Surveying (20 participants) and interviewing (4 user interviews)

We curated a quick screen survey, then followed up with interviews that allowed us to understand people's motivations and barriers in joining and contributing to an organization. We then synthesized our research by utilizing affinity mapping that allowed us to spot tends in our data. From our research we learned a few key insights about our users. From there we developed our main key persona that we designed for.

Key Insights

  • They needed to feel alignment with the cause and have trust in an organization
  • They lacked of time, money, and educational resources
  • They wanted to be more involved but are unsure where to start
  • They felt like they could not make a difference

Who is the Busy Outsider

Someone who is curious about preventing food waste, but is unsure where to start or how they can have an impact.

Needs

  • Reduce amount of food they waste
  • Help people in need an the environment
  • Assure sure their time is worthwhile
    and going to a trustworthy organization

Pain Points

  • Lacks time to contribute
  • Wastes excess food

Designing Fast With Purpose

Once we built a strong research foundation we then started our first initial redesign of Food Runners website. Once we had our low-fi wireframes we then conducted usability testing. Conducting usability testing at an early stage was very beneficial for our design process because it provided us with great insights on what features to actually implement in our design.

  • The labeling of the navigation was kind of confusing to the users
  • The placement of page titles did not convey the sense of a new page

Once we iterated and fixed some the problems that were addressed we then stared to move into medium to Hi-fidelity wireframes.

Hi-Fi Wire Frames

CALL TO ACTION

Since Food runners current website did not have any clear call to action we included two main call to actions on the landing screen. By having a clear call to action it will grab the users attention and let them know what they should be focusing on. As for color since food runners branding is red we really wanted to choose a color that compliments red and also entails the feeling of encouragement. This would also encourage user to contribute to the cause which satisfies both the user and organization needs

MISSION STATEMENT AND OUR IMPACT

Since trust was very important to our users when deciding whether or not to join an organization we knew we had to address it in our redesign. Some features that we used to establish trust were...

  • The mission statement on the homepage to allow users to know what Food runners mission is right when they get on the site
  • Our Impact on the landing page showcasing facts and statistics about the impact Food Runners has made in the community
  • List of Food Runners established business partners
  • Our stories page featuring testimonials from people impacted by or involved with Food Runners
  • About us page to give users a deeper understanding of Food Runners

IMPROVING THE DONATION FLOW

Our users also expressed that they lacked time to donate so we wanted to improve the existing flow of donating to Food Runners. We redesigned the current input method and gave the users predetermined donation amounts that make the flow faster and more convenient . We also incorporated supporting microcopy that allowed the users to know the impact they are going to have

Getting New Users Involved

We designed a take action page that allowed users who want to be more involved but are unsure where to start a page to get informed. On the get started page they would be able to learn more about hunger issues and the food waste movement. We also wanted to incorporate easy ways that the user would be able to get involved an have an impact in their community.
